Street art

in Czech-Republic
  • Dorian
  • Feb 13, 2023


Ostrava, a city in the eastern part of the Czech Republic, is known for its rich industrial history and culture. However, in recent years, it has also become a hub for street art, with a growing number of murals and other street art installations popping up around the city. One of the most notable street art projects in Ostrava is the Ostrava Street Art Festival, which has been held annually since 2013. The festival brings together artists from around the world to create large-scale murals and other street art installations throughout the city. These pieces are often inspired by the city's industrial heritage, with many incorporating elements of machinery and other industrial themes. One of the most famous pieces of street art in Ostrava is the "Industrial Madonna," a large mural of the Virgin Mary holding a steel beam that has become an iconic symbol of the city's industrial past. The mural was created by the Spanish artist Okuda San Miguel as part of the 2017 Ostrava Street Art Festival, and has since become a popular tourist attraction. Another notable piece of street art in Ostrava is the "Wall of Fame," a series of murals featuring portraits of famous Czech and Slovak figures. The murals were created by the Czech artist Jan Karpíšek, and can be found on the side of a building near the city's main train station. In addition to these large-scale installations, Ostrava is also home to a number of smaller street art pieces, such as stencils, stickers, and wheatpastes. These pieces can be found throughout the city, often in unexpected places such as alleyways and abandoned buildings. While street art has become increasingly popular in Ostrava in recent years, it is not without controversy. Some residents have criticized the murals and other installations for being too modern and out of place in a city with such a strong industrial heritage. Others have expressed concern about the potential for graffiti and other forms of vandalism to damage the city's historic buildings and landmarks. Despite these concerns, street art has continued to flourish in Ostrava, with new installations popping up on a regular basis. Many residents and visitors have embraced the art form as a way to bring new life to the city's streets and public spaces, and to celebrate its unique industrial heritage. In conclusion, street art has become a significant part of Ostrava's cultural landscape in recent years, with a growing number of murals and other installations appearing throughout the city. While some have criticized the art form for being too modern and out of place in a city with such a strong industrial heritage, many others have embraced it as a way to celebrate the city's unique history and bring new life to its streets and public spaces. With the continued growth of the Ostrava Street Art Festival and other initiatives, it is likely that street art will continue to play an important role in the city's cultural and artistic scene for years to come.
