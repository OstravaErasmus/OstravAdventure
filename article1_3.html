Dolní Vítkovice

The Iron Castle
  • Richard
  • Mar 13, 2023

In France we have the Eiffel Tower but in the Czech Republic and more precisely in Ostrava there is the steel castle, Dolní Vítkovice.

Image of DOLNÍ VÍTKOVICE

Dolní Vítkovice includes the vast industrial complex of the Vítkovice steelworks. It is made up of three blocks, sometimes nicknamed the "Hradčany of Ostrava". In the northern part is the Hlubina mine which, as a source of fuel for the blast furnaces, was to be connected to the whole complex. The blast furnaces, the coking plant and its chemical operation are connected by conveyor belts, loading devices and transport bridges. The central part concentrates mechanical manufacturing, still in working order, and in the southern part a set of administrative and industrial buildings is also worth a visit.

Hlubina mine

In the complex you will learn about the steel industry in the Ostrava region while having fun. If you start near the blast furnace, the discovery route will guide you along the path that the raw materials once followed. A bucket lift will transport you to the mouth of the furnace, which once filled with iron ore, coke, calcite and other additives. You can even get to the top of a tower that will give you an imposing view of the whole city of Ostrava, and on the way back you can see the bowels of the oven, where the temperature exceeded 1500 degrees Celsius .

Bolt Tower & the Blast Furnace

The first impetus for the creation of the Vítkovice ironworks was given in 1828 by the Archbishop of Olomouc Rudolph. Over time, six ovens were put into operation. The entire Vítkovice site is now undergoing a major revitalization. During the reconstruction, the giant gasometer will be transformed, for example, into an original multi-purpose hall. In the near future, an industrial museum and the World of Technology, which will be an original place of education and leisure for the whole family, will see the light of day in the complex.
