Ostrava zoo

in Czech-Republic

  • Dorian
  • Mar 6, 2023


The Ostrava Zoo, located in the city of Ostrava in the Czech Republic, is one of the largest and most popular zoos in the country. Spread over an area of 110 hectares, the zoo is home to over 4000 animals from around the world, making it a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. The Ostrava Zoo was founded in 1951, and has since become known for its commitment to conservation and animal welfare. The zoo's collection includes a wide range of animals, from large mammals such as elephants, lions, and tigers, to reptiles, birds, and even insects. Many of the animals at the zoo are endangered, and the zoo plays an important role in conservation efforts through breeding programs and other initiatives. One of the most popular exhibits at the Ostrava Zoo is the African savannah, which features a variety of animals from the African continent, including zebras, giraffes, and ostriches. Visitors can also observe lions and tigers in their natural habitats, and watch as elephants roam the vast savannahs. Another popular exhibit at the Ostrava Zoo is the Amazon jungle, which showcases the diverse plant and animal life of the rainforest. Visitors can walk through a replica of a jungle habitat, and observe animals such as monkeys, parrots, and sloths up close. The Ostrava Zoo also has a number of interactive exhibits and activities for visitors, including a petting zoo where children can feed and interact with farm animals, and a playground where children can play and learn about animals. In addition to its animal exhibits, the Ostrava Zoo is also known for its beautiful gardens and natural landscapes. The zoo's extensive grounds include walking paths, picnic areas, and scenic views of the surrounding countryside. Despite its popularity, the Ostrava Zoo has faced criticism from animal rights activists who argue that zoos are inherently cruel and unnatural environments for animals. The zoo has responded to these concerns by implementing measures to improve the welfare of its animals, such as providing larger habitats and more natural environments. In conclusion, the Ostrava Zoo is one of the largest and most popular zoos in the Czech Republic, and is known for its commitment to conservation and animal welfare. Its extensive collection of animals from around the world, interactive exhibits, and beautiful gardens make it a popular destination for tourists and locals alike. While it has faced criticism from animal rights activists, the zoo has taken steps to improve the welfare of its animals and continues to play an important role in conservation efforts.
